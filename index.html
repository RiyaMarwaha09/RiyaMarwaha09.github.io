About Me

My journey into business analytics began with one question: how can data drive smarter decisions? Over the past few years, I’ve explored this through projects- building fraud detection models on 61K+ transactions to separate genuine from fraudulent activity, designing retail dashboards that revealed price mismatch as the top driver of returns, and developing marketing dashboards that showed where ad spend truly pays off. Each project wasn’t just about models and metrics, but about uncovering patterns businesses could act on immediately.

What drives me is the impact behind the numbers — knowing that the insights I create help reduce risks, cut inefficiencies, and guide smarter strategies. Beyond projects, I’ve led as a Graduate Assistant, Tutor, and Orientation Leader — roles that taught me how analysis and leadership can go hand in hand. My motivation is simple: I want to be the Business Analyst who not only interprets data, but transforms it into decisions that make organizations more confident, efficient, and ready for growth.

Processed 2,176 boutique records using SQL, R, and Tableau to uncover why customers returned products. Logistic regression revealed price mismatch as the strongest predictor of returns, while Tableau dashboards showed certain categories like apparel faced higher return risks with negative margins. These insights highlighted opportunities to reduce return-related costs by ~15% and improve inventory optimization.
